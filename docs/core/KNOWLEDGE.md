# Legalization Framework

## IMPORTANT

The correct legalization process is determined by BOTH:

1. the country where the document originated

AND

2. the destination country where the document will be used.

Never determine legalization requirements using only one of these factors.

---

## General Principle

Origin Country
+
Destination Country
=
Correct Legalization Path

Different combinations may require:

- Apostille
- Authentication
- Embassy Legalization
- Consular Legalization
- Other country-specific procedures

---

## Editorial Rule

Destination pages should primarily discuss the legalization process that applies to Hive's most common customers.

For example:

China pages primarily discuss Apostilles because most Hive China clients present documents issued in Hague Convention member countries.

Vietnam pages primarily discuss Authentication because those same origin countries require Authentication for Vietnam.

Do not imply every client follows the same legalization process.

When exceptions exist, explain them.

# Terminology

## Apostille

Use when describing documents that require Apostilles.

Preferred wording:

- Obtain an Apostille
- Degree Apostille
- FBI Background Check Apostille
- Marriage Certificate Apostille
- Apostille Services

Avoid using Apostille as a verb whenever possible.

Prefer:

"We obtain an Apostille for your degree."

instead of

"We apostille your degree."

---

## Authentication

Use when Authentication is the correct legalization process.

Preferred wording:

- Embassy Authentication
- Document Authentication
- Embassy Legalization

Do not use Authentication on pages where Apostille is the primary process.

## Typical Teacher Documentation

Teachers relocating to China commonly require:

- Criminal Background Check
- Degree
- Teaching Qualification (TEFL / TESOL / CELTA) where applicable

For documents issued in Hague Convention countries, these documents generally require Apostilles before use in China.

Always verify employer requirements before recommending services.

---

## Name Verification

One of Hive's highest consultation priorities is verifying that names match across all documents.

Compare the applicant's passport against:

- Background Check
- Degree
- Teaching Qualification
- Marriage Certificate (where applicable)

Pay particular attention to:

- Missing middle names
- Initials
- Hyphenated names
- Maiden names

Some Chinese authorities may reject documents if names do not exactly match the passport.

Where discrepancies exist:

- Consult HR or the employer.
- Confirm local acceptance before processing.
- Where appropriate, include a notarial statement confirming both names refer to the same individual.

Never assume a mismatch will be accepted.

---

## Teaching Qualifications

Teachers with less than two years of documented teaching experience will generally require an apostilled teaching qualification.

Applicants with substantial documented teaching experience may not require one.

Always confirm employer requirements before recommending an additional certificate.

When required, the qualification should normally indicate at least 120 hours of training.

---

## Background Check Selection

Selecting the correct background check is one of Hive's primary consultation services.

Do not automatically recommend an FBI report.

Recommendations should consider:

- Employer requirements
- Application city
- Local authority requirements
- Applicant nationality
- Applicant location
- Processing timeline

Whenever possible, recommend the least burdensome document that satisfies all known requirements.

State-level police checks are often acceptable and significantly faster than national fingerprint-based reports, but they should only be recommended when they satisfy employer and local authority requirements.

---

## Cities Commonly Requesting National / Federal Reports

Some Chinese cities are known to request national-level police certificates rather than state or provincial reports.

Known examples include:

- Ningbo
- Xiamen
- Kunshan
- Suzhou
- Dalian

This list is not exhaustive.

Requirements change periodically and should always be confirmed.

---

## Canadian Applicants

Where national fingerprint-based police certificates are required, recommend RCMP rather than CPIC.

Always verify current requirements before advising clients.

---

## Chinese Permanent Residence

Applicants applying for Chinese Permanent Residence generally require national-level police certificates.

Typical examples include:

United States
- FBI Identity History Summary

Canada
- RCMP Certified Criminal Record Check

United Kingdom
- ACRO Police Certificate

Always verify current requirements before beginning document preparation.

---

## Consultation Philosophy

Hive begins every consultation by understanding the client's circumstances before recommending documents.

Typical consultation questions include:

- Which country are you moving to?
- What nationality are you?
- Where are you currently located?
- Which city will you be working in?
- What has your employer requested?
- Which documents do you already have?
- What is your deadline?

Only after these questions are answered should document recommendations be made.
