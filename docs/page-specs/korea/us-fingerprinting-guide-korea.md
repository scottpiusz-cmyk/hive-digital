# Page Specification: US Fingerprinting Guide for Korea-Based Applicants

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft specification; not approved copy

Proposed route: `/us-fingerprinting-guide-korea/`

Page role: Supporting process guide

Priority: P0

## Objective

Explain how an applicant in South Korea can prepare for fingerprint submission
when requesting a US FBI Identity History Summary or another US record.

This page should solve a practical problem without claiming that Hive currently
operates a Korean fingerprint location or that one fingerprint format works for
every agency.

## Audience and Intent

- US citizens living in South Korea who need an FBI check
- Korea-based applicants asked to submit US fingerprint cards
- schools, recruiters, and HR teams assisting candidates
- users comparing electronic capture, printed cards, and ink fingerprints

## Critical Distinctions

- The receiving agency determines acceptable card, image, identity, signature,
  and submission requirements.
- Electronic capture does not automatically mean electronic submission to the
  FBI; prints may be captured digitally and printed onto a physical card.
- A fingerprint provider captures prints; it does not decide whether the record
  will satisfy a Korean employer or authority.
- FBI, state, licensing, and other checks may require different procedures.

## Proposed Page Structure

1. **Hero**
   - Fingerprint planning for US checks while in South Korea.
   - CTA: Confirm the destination and receiving agency first.
2. **Identify the check before booking fingerprints**
   - FBI Identity History Summary.
   - State or local record.
   - Professional licensing or other purpose.
3. **Confirm the receiving specification**
   - Required card or form.
   - Rolled and plain impressions.
   - Identity and technician fields.
   - Physical card versus permitted electronic path.
4. **Capture options**
   - Law-enforcement or private fingerprint provider where available.
   - Ink capture.
   - Live-scan capture printed to an accepted card, when supported.
   - Participating US Post Office option applies only in the circumstances
     described by the FBI; do not imply it is available in Korea.
5. **Quality checklist**
   - Complete required biographical fields accurately.
   - Follow the receiving agency's current instructions.
   - Consider multiple cards when the official guidance or provider recommends
     it for rejection risk.
   - Protect cards and personal data in transit.
6. **What happens after capture**
   - Submission, result, and possible apostille are separate steps.
7. **Privacy and safety**
   - Fingerprints are highly sensitive personal data.
   - Do not email images or upload them through an unapproved website form.
8. **FAQ and consultation CTA**
