# Page Specification: US TEFL/TESOL Certificate Apostille for South Korea

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft specification; not approved copy

Proposed route: `/us-tefl-tesol-apostille-korea/`

Page role: Certificate-specific qualification page

Priority: P2

## Objective

Help applicants determine whether a TEFL/TESOL certificate was actually
requested, who issued it, and whether that document can follow an apostille path
for its intended use in South Korea.

This page must not claim that every Korea teaching role or visa requires a
TEFL/TESOL certificate, or that every private certificate is eligible for an
apostille in the same way.

## Audience and Intent

- educators whose school or recruiter requested a TEFL/TESOL certificate
- certificate holders unsure whether the issuer, notarization, or state matters
- schools, recruiters, and HR teams preparing candidate instructions

## Core Decision Points

1. Who requested the certificate and is it a legal, employer, programme, or
   recruiter requirement?
2. Who issued the certificate and where is that entity legally located?
3. Is the document an original certificate, electronic certificate, provider
   letter, notarized copy, or another record?
4. Can the relevant state authority apostille the proposed document or notarial
   act?
5. Is the requester asking for the TEFL/TESOL certificate in addition to a
   degree or background check?
6. Is translation or a particular delivery format separately requested?

## Proposed Page Structure

1. **Hero and qualification statement**
   - Confirm whether the certificate is needed before ordering a service.
2. **TEFL/TESOL is not the same as a university degree**
   - Explain separate issuers and document paths.
3. **Identify the issuing entity and state**
   - Online delivery does not by itself identify the competent jurisdiction.
4. **Confirm an apostilleable document path**
   - Obtain current issuer and state guidance.
   - Avoid promising eligibility before document review.
5. **How this fits the broader Korea document package**
   - Degree, background check, and teaching certificate are separate items.
   - The requester decides which are relevant.
6. **For schools and recruiters**
   - State whether TEFL/TESOL is required or preferred.
   - Specify accepted issuer, course, document, and authentication format when
     the organization has such a policy.
7. **Common avoidable mistakes**
   - Apostilling in the applicant's state rather than the document's jurisdiction.
   - Assuming every PDF certificate can be apostilled.
   - Purchasing authentication before confirming the certificate is requested.
8. **FAQ and consultation CTA**

## Source Requirements

- Written requirement from the school, recruiter, programme, or authority
- Certificate issuer's current record and verification policy
- Current notarial and apostille instructions from the relevant state authority
- Current Korea-side primary source if the copy makes a visa-related statement

## Claims Requiring Approval

- that a TEFL/TESOL certificate is required for any visa or role
- issuer eligibility or state apostille eligibility
- states and certificate providers Hive can support
- notarization, translation, timing, price, courier, or acceptance claims

## Acceptance Criteria

- Clearly distinguishes a private teaching certificate from a university degree.
- Does not present TEFL/TESOL as a universal Korea requirement.
- Includes a content-value check to prevent a thin or cannibalizing page.
- Does not guarantee apostille eligibility, acceptance, employment, or visa
  outcome.
- Passes source, operational, metadata, internal-link, sitemap, privacy, mobile,
  and desktop review.
