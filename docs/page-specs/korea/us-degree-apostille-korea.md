# Page Specification: US Degree Apostille for South Korea

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft specification; not approved copy

Proposed route: `/us-degree-apostille-korea/`

Page role: Degree-specific service page

Priority: P1

## Objective

Help US degree holders and hiring organizations identify the correct issuing
state, document format, and apostille path for an academic credential intended
for use in South Korea.

The page must not imply that an apostilled diploma alone satisfies every Korean
visa, employer, school, university, or professional requirement.

## Audience and Intent

- educators and professionals using a US degree in South Korea
- international schools, universities, recruiters, and HR teams
- applicants who need to understand original, copy, notarization, transcript,
  and apostille distinctions

## Core Decision Points

1. Which institution issued the degree and in which state?
2. What exact document did the requester name: diploma, degree certificate,
   transcript, registrar letter, or certified copy?
3. Must a copy be notarized or certified before the state apostille step?
4. Does the requesting organization require a sealed or separately issued
   transcript?
5. Is translation requested, and by whom?
6. Does the applicant have a deadline that should be discussed without a
   guaranteed completion date?

## Proposed Page Structure

1. **Hero**
   - US academic documents for use in South Korea.
   - CTA: Confirm the institution, state, and requested format.
2. **Apostille the document in its issuing jurisdiction**
   - Explain the issuing-state principle in plain language.
3. **Degree, copy, and transcript are not interchangeable**
   - Describe the questions to ask the requester.
4. **Typical decision path**
   - Obtain written requirement.
   - Confirm institution and state.
   - Determine acceptable document format.
   - Complete any required certification or notarization.
   - Apply to the competent state apostille authority.
   - Complete only separately confirmed translation or delivery steps.
5. **For schools, recruiters, and HR**
   - Specify document type and format in candidate instructions.
   - Plan exception handling for institutions or states with different rules.
6. **Common avoidable mistakes**
   - Apostilling in the applicant's current state instead of the issuing state.
   - Assuming a scan or ordinary photocopy is acceptable.
   - Treating a transcript and diploma as the same document.
   - Starting before the requester confirms the format.
7. **FAQ and consultation CTA**
