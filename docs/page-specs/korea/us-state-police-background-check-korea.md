# Page Specification: US State Police Background Checks for South Korea

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft specification; not approved copy

Proposed route: `/us-state-police-background-check-korea/`

Page role: Qualification and service page

Priority: P1

## Objective

Help users determine whether a state-issued police or criminal-record document
was specifically requested for use in South Korea and explain why it is not the
same product as an FBI Identity History Summary.

This page must prevent users from buying a cheaper or faster state service when
the requester actually requires a national FBI record. It should also avoid the
opposite mistake: selling an FBI workflow when a legitimate state record is the
document expressly requested.

## Audience and Intent

- applicants whose employer or checklist names a US state record
- people comparing state police checks with FBI checks
- schools, recruiters, and HR teams interpreting candidate requirements
- users who need to authenticate or apostille a state-issued record

## Core Decision Points

1. Which state and agency is named by the requester?
2. Is the requested search name based, fingerprint based, or either?
3. Does the requester require statewide or national scope?
4. What original, certification, notarization, or signature is required before
   the issuing state can apostille the document?
5. Is the record intended for a government filing, employer screening, school
   policy, or another purpose?
6. Is translation or a particular delivery format separately requested?

## Proposed Page Structure

1. **Hero and warning against substitution**
   - State and FBI records are different.
   - CTA: Send the written requirement for review, without sending the record.
2. **When a state record may be relevant**
   - Only conditional examples, not universal Korea requirements.
3. **State check versus FBI check**
   - Issuing authority.
   - Geographic scope.
   - Search method.
   - Apostille authority.
   - Receiving-organization decision.
4. **State-specific workflow**
   - Identify the correct state agency.
   - Obtain that agency's current instructions.
   - Confirm whether certification or notarization is needed.
   - Use the competent state authority for an apostille when applicable.
5. **Multi-state history**
   - Do not prescribe multiple reports; direct the user back to the requester.
6. **For HR and recruiters**
   - Write the required issuing authority and scope clearly.
   - Avoid vague wording such as "US police check."
7. **FAQ and consultation CTA**

## Source Requirements

- The current website or written guidance of the relevant state police,
  identification bureau, court, or records authority
- The relevant Secretary of State or designated apostille authority
- [US Department of State: Criminal Records Checks](https://travel.state.gov/en/international-travel/living-abroad/criminal-records.html)
- Written requirements from the Korean requester or responsible authority

Do not create one generic fifty-state procedure. State issuing and apostille
requirements must be checked case by case and dated.

## Claims Requiring Approval

- states Hive can directly serve
- fingerprint, name-search, notarization, certification, or apostille workflow
  for any named state
- time, price, validity, acceptance, or delivery claims
- whether a specific Korean visa, employer, school, or authority accepts a state
  report

## Acceptance Criteria

- Clearly states that a state check is not an FBI report.
- Leads with requirement confirmation before purchase.
- Uses no generic state workflow as if it applies nationally.
- Contains no guaranteed acceptance, timeline, cost, or immigration outcome.
- Final copy cites the relevant state authority for every state-specific claim.
- Passes privacy, metadata, internal-link, sitemap, desktop, and mobile checks.
