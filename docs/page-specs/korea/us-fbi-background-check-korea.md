# Page Specification: US FBI Background Check for South Korea

Version: 0.1

Status: Draft specification; not approved copy

Proposed route: `/us-fbi-background-check-korea/`

Page role: Primary strategic service page

Priority: P0

## Objective

Help US applicants and Korea-based hiring organizations understand the decision
path for obtaining and, when required, apostilling an FBI Identity History
Summary for use in South Korea.

The page should convert qualified cases to consultation while distinguishing an
FBI result from state or local police records and avoiding universal visa or
employer claims.

## Audience and Intent

- US citizens applying for Korea-based teaching or other international roles
- applicants already outside the United States
- schools, universities, recruiters, and HR teams coordinating US candidates
- users searching for an FBI check, FBI apostille, or Korea background-check
  support

## Core Decision Points

The content should ask:

1. Who requested the record and for what purpose?
2. Did the requester specify an FBI record or another police certificate?
3. Is the applicant eligible to use a direct FBI submission or a channeler?
4. Where will fingerprints be taken and in what accepted format?
5. Is an apostille required for this case?
6. What format, issue-date window, translation, and delivery instructions did
   the requester provide?

## Verified Baseline

The FBI calls the record an **Identity History Summary Check**. Its official FAQ
states that the request is fingerprint based, that direct electronic and mail
options exist, and that applicants may also use an FBI-approved channeler. The
FBI authenticates its search results; when an apostille is necessary, the
requester may submit the FBI-authenticated result to the US Department of State.

These facts do not establish that every Korea-bound applicant needs the same
record, format, issue date, or apostille. Korea-side requirements must be
confirmed for the applicant's actual requester and filing path.

## Proposed Page Structure

1. **Hero and qualification statement**
   - FBI background-check support for documents intended for South Korea.
   - CTA: Ask Hive to confirm the document path.
2. **Is an FBI check the document being requested?**
   - Explain FBI versus state/local record scope.
   - Tell users to obtain the request in writing when possible.
3. **The workflow at a glance**
   - Confirm requirements.
   - Choose a permitted submission method.
   - Capture acceptable fingerprints.
   - Obtain the FBI result.
   - Complete an apostille only when required.
   - Arrange any approved translation or delivery steps.
4. **Direct FBI versus approved channeler**
   - Describe choices without inventing turnaround or pricing.
   - Link to the current official channeler list.
5. **Applying from outside the United States**
   - Fingerprint logistics and format must be confirmed.
   - Do not promise a local Korea fingerprint location until capability is
     approved.
6. **Apostille distinction**
   - FBI document is federal.
   - State-issued records follow a different authority path.
7. **For schools, recruiters, and HR**
   - Consistent written requirements and candidate intake.
   - Exception handling through a human review.
8. **FAQ and consultation CTA**

## Claims Requiring Approval

- Hive access to or relationship with an FBI-approved channeler
- eligibility restrictions for a particular channeler
- turnaround, price, courier, translation, or expedited-service claims
- local fingerprint coverage in Korea
- document-age or format requirements
- any statement that a visa, employer, or authority will accept the result

## Acceptance Criteria

- Uses the official document name and does not describe the FBI as issuing an
  apostille.
- Distinguishes direct FBI submission, channelers, fingerprints, and apostille.
- Does not present a state record as an FBI substitute.
- Does not guarantee timing, price, acceptance, or visa outcome.
- Includes a source review date in final page governance metadata.
- Meets canonical, sitemap, structured-data, mobile, privacy, and contact-flow
  checks before publication.
