## Core Positioning

Hive Digital helps individuals and businesses prepare, obtain, authenticate,
legalize, translate, and move documents across borders. The site should reduce
confusion around document processes and provide a clear route to a consultation.

The company is a document-services and coordination provider. Do not present it
as a government authority, law firm, immigration authority, or guarantor of an
application outcome.

## Priority Audiences

- Expats and former expats who live or previously lived in China
- Individuals moving to, from, or within APAC for work, study, family, or
  immigration purposes
- International school teachers and education professionals
- Recruiters, HR teams, and international schools managing candidate documents
- Employers hiring internationally or onboarding staff in China
- Foreign companies establishing or operating a China entity
- Individuals who need background checks, police clearances, apostilles,
  legalization, certified translation, or fingerprinting

- FBI Identity History Summary Checks

- Other supported jurisdictional checks where confirmed

### Apostille And Authentication

- Hague Apostille coordination

- Notarization and certified translation coordination

### China Police Clearance

- China Police Clearance Certificates / Non-Criminal Record Certificates
- Current site coverage includes Beijing, Shanghai, Shenzhen, and other cities
  subject to individual eligibility and local availability

  visa, and city-specific evidence

### Fingerprinting

- FBI-compliant fingerprint capture
- Traditional ink-based fingerprinting
- Current site locations: Shanghai, Beijing, and Chengdu
- Other locations must be described as subject to appointment and confirmation

### China Visa And Work-Permit Documents

- Document preparation and review
- Translation and certification coordination
- Residence-permit and work-permit document support
- Do not imply guaranteed visa, permit, or government approval

### China Business Support

- WFOE and joint-venture registration support
- Business license, company chop, and bank-account coordination
- HR, payroll, accounting, tax-compliance, office-selection, and operational
  support where confirmed for the engagement

## Brand Voice

- Professional, calm, practical, and easy to understand
- Helpful to expats and international teams without sounding simplistic
- Specific and confident, but never absolute where government discretion applies
- Plain English first; explain specialist terms when they are necessary
- Avoid corporate filler, legalistic padding, fear-based selling, and exaggerated
  urgency
- Prefer clear next steps over broad promotional claims

## Messaging Priorities

1. Clarify what document or process the service supports.
2. Explain who the service is for and when they commonly need it.
3. Identify major requirements, dependencies, and location limitations.
4. Explain what Hive Digital coordinates without guaranteeing the outcome.
5. End with a direct consultation or contact action.

## Bilingual Rules

- English and Chinese pages should present the same core service facts and
  contact details.
- Translate for clarity and natural usage, not word for word.
- When changing service order, availability, contact information, or a major
  claim, inspect the corresponding page in the other language.
- Do not create a Chinese mirror automatically when the owner has requested an
  English-only campaign page or article.

## SEO Rules

- Preserve page-specific titles, descriptions, canonicals, language alternates,
  structured data, and sitemap coverage.
- Write for the user first; do not stuff location or service keywords.
- Use one clear search intent per landing page.
- Avoid duplicate pages that compete for the same service and geography.
- Government rules and processing conditions change. Date-sensitive guidance
  should identify its review date and be checked before publication.

## Content And Compliance Guardrails

- Do not guarantee processing time, approval, certificate issuance, visa or work
  permit approval, or acceptance by an authority.
- Do not provide individualized legal, immigration, or tax advice as definitive
  professional advice.
- Do not expose customer names, documents, identifiers, case details, or
  correspondence without explicit permission.
- Do not invent partnerships, certifications, government relationships,
  locations, customer counts, testimonials, or prices.
- Clearly mark city availability and document requirements as subject to review
  when local authority practice can vary.
